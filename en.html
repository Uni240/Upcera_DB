Warranty Obligations

Warranty is provided subject to operating conditions, such as the absence of moisture in the machine's air system and a stable power supply. For this, the laboratory must be equipped with a voltage stabilizer and an air dryer. Warranty obligations do not cover failures caused by improper operation or user actions.

2. Materials

2.1. Zirconia

DUO

Properties:

  • Translucency: 45% - 48%
  • Flexural Strength: 850 - 1200 MPa
  • Density after sintering: ≥6.0 g/cm³
  • CTE (25-500°C): (10.5±0.5)×10-6 K-1
  • Sintering Temperature: 1480°C

Sintering Program (Standard, up to 7 units):

  1. Step 1: Heat to 1000°C at 17°C/min.
  2. Step 2: Heat to 1300°C at 10°C/min.
  3. Step 3: Heat to 1515°C at 7°C/min.
  4. Step 4: Hold at 1515°C for 90 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Cool to 800°C at 8°C/min.
  6. Step 6: Natural cooling.

Sintering Program (For long-span bridges, >7 units):

  1. Step 1: Heat to 1300°C at 4°C/min.
  2. Step 2: Heat to 1515°C at 2°C/min.
  3. Step 3: Hold at 1515°C for 120 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Cool to 800°C at 4°C/min.
  5. Step 5: Natural cooling.
HT White

Properties:

  • Translucency: 39%
  • Flexural Strength: 1300 MPa
  • Sintering Temperature: 1530°C

Sintering Program:

  1. Step 1: Heat to 500°C at 3°C/min.
  2. Step 2: Heat to 1150°C at 8°C/min.
  3. Step 3: Hold at 1150°C for 30 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Heat to 1300°C at 2°C/min.
  5. Step 5: Heat to 1530°C at 4°C/min.
  6. Step 6: Hold at 1530°C for 120 minutes.
  7. Step 7: Cool to 800°C at 8°C/min, then natural cooling.
ST Series (White, Pre-shaded, Multilayer)

Properties:

  • Translucency: 43%
  • Flexural Strength: 1300 MPa
  • Sintering Temperature: 1530°C

Sintering Program (Standard):

Same as for HT White (peak temperature 1530°C).

Sintering Program (Fast, 4 hours):

  1. Step 1: Heat to 1150°C at 40°C/min.
  2. Step 2: Hold at 1150°C for 10 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Heat to 1550°C at 10°C/min.
  4. Step 4: Hold at 1550°C for 120 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Cool to 800°C at 40°C/min.

Sintering Program (Fast, 2 hours):

  1. Step 1: Heat to 1150°C at 50°C/min.
  2. Step 2: Heat to 1550°C at 8°C/min.
  3. Step 3: Hold at 1550°C for 30 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Cool to 800°C at 50°C/min.
TT-ONE Series (White, Pre-shaded, Multilayer)

Properties:

  • Translucency: 47%
  • Flexural Strength: 1000 MPa
  • Sintering Temperature: 1480°C (White/Pre-shaded), 1450°C (Multilayer)

Sintering Program (Standard):

  1. Step 1: Heat to 1150°C (in several stages).
  2. Step 2: Hold at 1150°C for 30 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Heat to 1480°C (or 1450°C) at 2-4°C/min.
  4. Step 4: Hold at peak temperature for 120 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Cool to 800°C at 8°C/min.
TT Series (White, Multilayer)

Properties:

  • Translucency: 49%
  • Flexural Strength: 600 MPa
  • Sintering Temperature: 1450°C

Sintering Program (Standard):

Same as for TT-ONE Multilayer (peak temperature 1450°C).

Explore Series (Functional, Esthetic, Hybrid)

Properties:

  • Translucency: 43% - 48.8% (depending on type)
  • Flexural Strength: 727 - 1300 MPa (gradient)
  • Sintering Temperature: 1480°C

Sintering Program (Standard):

Same as for TT-ONE (peak temperature 1480°C), but hold time for Hybrid is 180 minutes.

Tips
For stable furnace operation, a voltage with a pure sine wave without surges is required—we recommend using a UPS and a stabilizer. Before turning on, ensure the room is dry and clean. If the furnace has not been used for more than 24 hours, a drying cycle is mandatory—200 °C for 1 hour, otherwise, cracking of the ceramic or degradation of the heating elements is possible.

Clean the chamber at least once a week—use either a special cleaning powder or load unprocessed pieces of zirconia to absorb contaminants.

Any zirconia requires drying under an IR lamp for at least 20 minutes before sintering, especially if it is colored. Moisture causes microcracks and structural defects.

Do not use household hair dryers or ovens to dry zirconia. Only an IR lamp with diffused heat, at a distance of no closer than 20 cm.

After each sintering cycle, let the furnace cool down to <100 °C with the door closed. Opening a hot chamber can disrupt the temperature profile and deform the product.

Perform a calibration cycle according to the manufacturer's instructions every 2–3 months. This helps maintain heating uniformity and sintering accuracy.
